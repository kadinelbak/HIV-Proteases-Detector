Numeralizing Chemistry: Morgan Fingerprints
SMILES strings are transformed into high-dimensional Morgan fingerprints, encoding chemical structure for machine learning. This enables scalable, automated screening of vast chemical libraries—far beyond the reach of traditional wet-lab assays.

The Brain: Deep Learning Theory
A multilayer perceptron (MLP) learns to map molecular fingerprints to inhibition probability. Each layer transforms the representation, extracting higher-order features.

The Build: Tackling Imbalance
The MoleculeNet HIV dataset is highly imbalanced: 3.5% Active vs 96.5% Inactive. To overcome this, we implemented a weighted binary cross-entropy loss—amplifying the signal from rare actives and ensuring robust learning.

Training vs Validation Loss

  • Model avoids overfitting and learns meaningful patterns.
